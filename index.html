🎯 Phase 5: Examiner-Oriented Strategy

Coding vs Theory Ratio

The instructor heavily emphasizes applied coding. While theory (definitions, differences) is asked (e.g., "What is inheritance?", "Primitive vs Non-primitive"), the bulk of marks relies on writing full classes (e.g., `Student`, `CD` classes) and methods based on specific constraints.

Output & Dry-Run Prominence

Output prediction is highly common. You must be able to trace constructors (e.g., the `Puppy` and `Student` constructor questions) and understand string concatenations and scanner inputs during runtime.

OOP Pillars Deep-Dive

Inheritance is frequently tested both theoretically (class diagrams) and practically. Encapsulation (access specifiers) is tested. Abstraction/Polymorphism are in the lecture sheets but appeared slightly less explicitly in the provided paper snippets compared to basic class/object creation.

Out-of-Syllabus Warning

Basic Java structures (Loops, `Scanner`, Primitive data types) are expected knowledge. They appear in exams (e.g., separating digits, printing strings 9 times) but are Not in Current OOP Lecture Syllabus. Review them independently.
